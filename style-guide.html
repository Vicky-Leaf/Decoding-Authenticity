What's IKAT Technique?

The term Ikat comes from the Malay word ‘Mengikat.' This means to tie or to bind and refers to the process by which the pattern is created. The most important is the whole process is made by hand, which means integrating hand-dyeing and handwoven.

In Asia, the technique of Ikat has different names from diverse countries. It is known as ‘Patola’ in India, ‘Kasuri’ in Japan, ‘endek’ in Indonesia, 'Khan atlas' in Uzbek, etc.

The motifs from these regions are all different and have their characteristic. This is because motifs of handicraft in textile usually contains special moral and cultural heritages that are related to local history and tradition

How to appreciate Tangible Values of Intangible Techniques?

Today, when we look at the same type of handicraft, we might perceive a commonality and pervasiveness of their surface and attributes, but if we really look into their nature the cultural values and derived symbols are all different.
In the context of globalization, we rarely think about how the final products connect us to different locations, as well as to other lived experiences and ways of thinking. At this time, tracing the authenticity of Handloom would help create a tangible difference in our world.
